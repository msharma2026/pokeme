# User Experience Summary

## App Description

Our app helps UC Davis students find other people to play sports with and organize pickup games. Users can browse player profiles, match with people who share similar interests, and join or create public meetups for sports like basketball, volleyball, and badminton.

## Quantitative Metrics

Total users interviewed: **10**

Average difficulty rating: **~2 / 5**

Users who joined at least one meetup: **5 / 10**

Users who matched or connected with another player: **6 / 10**

Users who said the app solved their problem: **most users**

## Key Insights

### Pain (Push)

The main problem users faced was **not having enough people to play sports with when their friends were unavailable**. Many users also mentioned difficulty finding players with **similar skill levels** or finding people interested in the same sport.

Examples:

* Mindy could not play volleyball because her group only had three players.
* Ethan wanted to play badminton but mostly saw experienced players in the gym.
* Vin struggled to find basketball players at his skill level.

### Promise

Most users expected the app to **help them quickly find players or meetups for sports at UC Davis**. Many were excited about the ability to:

* discover new sports partners
* join public meetups
* see compatibility scores
* connect with players who share similar schedules or interests

Users generally believed the concept was useful for **meeting new people and organizing games**.

### Anxiety

Several users had concerns that slowed them down when using the app.

The most common anxieties were:

* **Skill levels may not be accurate**
  (Users said profiles felt vague or subjective)

* **Uncertainty about whether people would show up**
  (Some meetups had fewer attendees than expected)

* **Confusion with certain features**

  * Discovery page browsing flow
  * Meaning of “match stats”
  * How poking works

* **Limited number of users**
  Some users worried the app would not be useful unless more students joined.

### Inertia

Many users said the **old method of finding players was still easier in some situations**.

Common alternatives included:

* Going to the ARC and joining pickup games
* Asking friends or classmates
* Joining sports clubs

Users felt the app would become more useful **once there were more active users and meetups available**.

## Magic Moment

The “magic moment” for users occurred when they **successfully found or joined a meetup**.

Examples include:

* Fabian joining a volleyball meetup.
* Feruza joining a basketball meetup she planned to attend.
* Vin finding a basketball game with players closer to his skill level.

At this point, users felt the app was actually solving their problem.

## Improvements Based on Feedback

Based on user feedback, we identified several improvements for the app:

1. **Improve profile information**

   * Provide clearer skill level indicators
   * Show more detailed bios for players

2. **Improve meetup coordination**

   * Add clearer group chat or coordination features
   * Show reliability or attendance indicators

3. **Improve discovery flow**

   * Make browsing profiles easier
   * Clarify how “poke” works

4. **Increase user activity**

   * Encourage more meetup creation
   * Add more sports options (e.g., pickleball, ping pong)

Overall, users liked the concept of the app and found it easy to use, but many said it would become significantly more valuable as the user base grows.
