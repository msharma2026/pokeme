# Teddy
interviewed Mindy.
Questions:
Walk me through the last time you tried to play a sports but didn’t have a team/partner. (Pain)

When you first opened the app, what did hope it would magically do for you? (Promise)

When you saw the player profiles and the AI output what made you doubtful? (friction)

If you had to stop using the app and go back to the old way, what would be easier? (Inertia)

Predictions:
Pain: social anxiety when it comes to playing with strangers
Promise: the app would connect the user to sports players and allow them to chat and schedule meetings
Anxiety: not sure about player levels accuracy 
Inertia: old way was faster because you just go to a court and find players

Answers:

Went to the arc with only 3 friends
Not enough people for a full game
Difference in skill level made it not enjoyable 

Hoped to be matched with a team with same skill level and played the same sport

Doesn’t trust skill levels: it is subjective, no way to prove it on the app
Trusts AI score because of detailed breakdown

Easier to do the old way if they already had a group to play with
Not having to find a schedule that matches with yours is easier because people’s schedules are different, you can just go to the arc and find players


Questions:

How many people did you connect with?
How many meetups did you join?
When was the last time you opened the app to look for a match?
Is the discovery page useful to you? Why or why not?

Answers:

Connected with 3 players 
Created one meetup for volleyball

Did not join any meetup, there were no volleyball meetups

Opened the app everyday to check if a volleyball meetup was created

Discover page is not as useful because it only shows one person at time and have to poke them, wait for them to poke back. Meetups are just so much faster. Need to connect with a lot of people to play a volleyball game.
