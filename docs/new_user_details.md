# Teddy
The user understood that it was a sports app right away but they needed more time to understand what exactly it does. Once they started clicking on the different tabs they understood that it was a social app for sports players. They mentioned that the UI was simple and easy to understand. They suggested making it possible to scroill through user profiles instead of "passing" one to see the next one. I asked them what they thought the main feature of the app was and they said it was the spontaneous meetups. I asked them if the "discovery" to find sport buddies was a feature that they found helpful and they said that it was good to have if they were looking for players of the same skill levels with whom they would be able to play with more consistently. Another suggestion was to implement a group chat feature for meetups so players can talk to each other before meeting in person. The only complaint they had was the lack of notifications when joining a meetup or getting poked by another user. 

## (DAVID) Interview 2 - Fabian
- Date/time: March 2, 2026, evening
- Who: UC Davis student, plays volleyball recreationally
- Phase 1 setup delivered: yes
- First glance (5-second): "Looks like a sports app to find people to play with at Davis."
- Task scenario given: "Imagine your friends are busy and you want to play volleyball this weekend. Show how you would use this app."
- What he clicked/said in order (raw notes):
  - Opened Discover first and browsed profiles
  - Expected to scroll through more profiles naturally
  - Sent a poke to one volleyball player
  - Switched to Meetups tab
  - Opened a volleyball meetup and joined it
  - Tried to find a group chat for that meetup
- Where he got confused/stuck (raw notes):
  - Profile browsing flow felt unclear (pass vs browse)
  - Could not clearly find meetup-wide group messaging
- Did he reach magic moment? (yes/no + how):
  - Yes; he found a relevant volleyball opportunity and joined it
- Vibe check:
  - Useful? "Yes, if it shows active players around my skill level."
  - Trust it? "Somewhat, I want to know why suggestions appear."
  - What would he do next? "Message people who seem active and confirm a game."
- Debrief:
  - Difficulty (1-5): 3
  - One thing to change: "Make profile browsing easier and add meetup group chat."
  - One-sentence app description: "It helps students quickly find people and pickup games for sports."

## (DAVID) Interview 3 - Feruza (Basketball)
- Date/time: March 2, 2026, evening
- Who: UC Davis student, plays basketball
- Phase 1 setup delivered: yes
- First glance (5-second): "This looks like an app for finding people to play basketball or other sports nearby."
- Task scenario given: "Imagine you want to play basketball tomorrow evening but don't have enough people. Show how you would use the app."
- What she clicked/said in order (raw notes):
  - Started in Discover and looked for basketball players
  - Checked profile details and wanted skill-level confidence
  - Sent a poke to one player
  - Opened Meetups and looked for basketball options
  - Joined a basketball meetup
  - Looked for a way to coordinate with all attendees after joining
- Where she got confused/stuck (raw notes):
  - Wanted clearer reliability/activity signals before committing
  - Wanted easier post-join coordination with the whole group
- Did she reach magic moment? (yes/no + how):
  - Yes; she found and joined a basketball meetup she said she would attend
- Vibe check:
  - Useful? "Yes, this is faster than asking random group chats."
  - Trust it? "Mostly, but I want confidence people will actually show up."
  - What would she do next? "Join, then message to confirm time and who is coming."
- Debrief:
  - Difficulty (1-5): 2
  - One thing to change: "Show reliability/activity indicators for players and meetups."
  - One-sentence app description: "A quick way for students to find real people for basketball and pickup sports."
